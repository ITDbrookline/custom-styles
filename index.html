The Brookline Health Department issues licenses to operate recreational camps for children.

A recreational camp is any day, primitive or outpost, residential, sports, travel or trip camp conducted wholly or in part for recreation or recreational instruction, which:

  • Operates for profit or philanthropic or charitable purposes, whether or not a fee is charged, and
  • Serves five or more children who are not members of the family or personal guests of the operator, and
  • Operates for any period of time between June 1st and September 30th of any year or fewer than 15 business days during any other time of the year.

Please consult (105 CMR 430.000, MA Regulations for Minimum Standards for Recreational Camps for Children, State Sanitary Code, Chapter IV) and all guidance documents prior to filling out the application. Additionally, contact the Department of Public Health, Bureau of Environmental Health, and Community Sanitation Program for any questions regarding the following documents:

  • Procedures for the background review of staff and volunteers [105 CMR 430.090]
  • A copy of promotional literature [105 CMR 430.190(C)]
    • Procedures for reporting suspected child abuse or neglect [105 CMR 430.093]
    • A camp health care policy [105 CMR 430.159(B)]
      • Health Care Consultant Agreement Form [105 CMR 430.159(B)]
      • A discipline policy [105 CMR 430.191]
      • A fire evacuation plan - approved by the local fire department [105 CMR 430.210(A)]
    • A written statement of compliance from the local fire department [105 CMR 430.215]
    • A Disaster/Emergency plan [105 CMR 430.210(B)]
    • A lost camper plan [105 CMR 430.210(C)]
  • A traffic control plan [105 CMR 430.210(D)]
  • For Day Camps - contingency plans [105 CMR 430.211]
  • A current certificate of inspection from the local building inspector [105 CMR 430.451]
  • A lost swimmer plan (when applicable) [105 CMR 430.210(C)]
  • Field Trip itinerary if/when Out of Camp Activity is applicable
